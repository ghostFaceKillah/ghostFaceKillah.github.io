Using emacs shortcuts for all sorts of stuff on Mac



Summary

Emacs shortcuts work (almost everywhere) in the Mac, as well as in bash, so it is good investment to learn them.


Workflow is a very personal thing


Most of the people I know are pretty opinionated on them. Back in the day you use to have holy wars between emacs and vi users. That's why I am pretty reluctant to use up my precious time writting htmls recommending stuff that you're not going to use anyway. But in this case the expected return seems to high to ignore it.


Emacs style key bindings work in most places on OSX

Whenever you can input text in OSX, there are very high chances that emacs keybindings will work, enabling you to perform many basic text manipulation tasks such as

  • jump between words and lines
  • jump to beginning of the line
  • deleting last word or char without reaching out to delete, weirding out your pinkie
  • hitting enter without moving your hand
  • copy pasting the text
  • swapping two last


Additionally, most of the shortcuts do not require moving fingers a lot around the keyboard, shaving off these precious miliseconds you can later reuse for impressing your friends with your newely

Opposed to fish shell, iTerm and similar stuff, these shortcuts work out of the box on vast majority of bash unixey terminals, no installation. You can use it not only on your Mac, not only sshing to AWS, but also on your grandma's RedHat installation from 1998.
